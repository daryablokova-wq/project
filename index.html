Introduction

“Il Tesoro dei Medici” was an exhibition held in Moscow in 2011. It ran from 20 May to 1 August 2011 and was organized as part of the cultural exchanges between Italy and Russia.

The exhibition presented the extraordinary decorative-art collection of the Medici family, the dynasty that ruled Florence and Tuscany during the Renaissance. The works came mainly from the Medici collections preserved in Florence.

The show focused not on paintings, but on the Medici passion for luxury objects and collecting, including jewellery, gold and silver works, and artistic objects that demonstrated the family’s wealth, taste, and political power.

A major theme of the exhibition was how the Medici used collecting as a way to express Renaissance ideals of culture, magnificence, and authority. The treasures reflected the tastes of rulers such as Cosimo I de' Medici and later members of the dynasty, including the final Medici heir Anna Maria Luisa de' Medici.

Our goal was to explore how the combination of SPARQL queries and Large Language Models (LLMs) can enrich an existing cultural heritage knowledge graph with new, meaningful content.

Despite its relevance, it is still underrepresented in structured knowledge graphs, which makes it a perfect candidate for enrichment through semantic technologies and AI-powered language models.
